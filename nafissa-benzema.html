 Karim Benzema

Karim Benzema started his senior career in 2005 with his local team Olympique Lyonnais, intermittently assisting them to three Ligue 1 championships.

He ended the season as the league’s leading goalscorer, winning his fourth league championship and his first Coupe de France, and was named the league’s Player of the Year and a member of the Team of the Year in 2008.

When he signed for Real Madrid in 2009 for 35 million pounds, it set a new record for a football transfer in France at the time. After finding it difficult to establish himself in his first season, he eventually established a consistent goalscoring rate with the team.

He later became a key member of the BBC trio, which included Gareth Bale and Cristiano Ronaldo, who helped Real Madrid win four consecutive UEFA Champions League championships from 2014 to 2018.

International career

He became the fifth-highest goalscorer in his country’s history after scoring his first goal in a friendly match against Austria.
