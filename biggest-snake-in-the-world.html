How to Find the Biggest Snake in the World and Why You Shouldnt

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Monday, July 29, 2024

If you are fascinated by snakes and want to see the biggest snake in the world, you might be tempted to go on an adventure and look for it.

But before you do that, you should know some facts about the largest snakes on Earth and why they are not easy or safe to find.

In this article, I will tell you everything you need to know about the biggest snake in the world, how to measure it, where to find it, and why you shouldn’t.

 What is the world’s largest snake recorded?

The world’s largest snake recorded is a matter of debate, as different sources may use different criteria to measure the size of a snake. Some may use length, some may use weight, and some may use both.

However, most experts agree that the two main contenders for the title of the world’s largest snake are the reticulated python and the green anaconda.

The reticulated python (Malayopython reticulatus) is widely considered to be the longest snake in the world, according to the Natural History Museum.

On average, this snake reaches approximately 6.25 meters, or 20.5 feet, in length.

The longest recorded reticulated python was discovered in 1912 and measured 10 meters, or about 32.8 feet, long.

However, this measurement has been disputed by some experts who claim that it was exaggerated or inaccurate.

The green anaconda (Eunectes murinus) is widely considered to be the heaviest snake in the world, according to National Geographic.

On average, this snake weighs about 227 kilograms or 550 pounds.

The heaviest recorded green anaconda was captured in 1944 and weighed 215 kilograms or 473 pounds.

However, this weight has also been challenged by some experts who claim that it was overestimated or inaccurate.

Both snakes are non-venomous constrictors that kill their prey by wrapping their bodies around them and squeezing them until they suffocate.

They can swallow large animals such as deer, pigs, crocodiles, and even humans.

They are found in tropical regions of Asia and South America, respectively.

Which is bigger anaconda or python?

The answer to this question depends on how you define “bigger”.

If you mean longer, then the python is bigger than the anaconda.

If you mean heavier, then the anaconda is bigger than the python.

You mean both length and weight combined, then it is hard to say which one is bigger, as there is no reliable way to compare them.

One of the reasons why it is difficult to compare the size of these snakes is that they are rarely measured accurately or scientifically.

Most of the records are based on anecdotal reports or unverified claims that may be exaggerated or inaccurate.

Moreover, snakes can vary in size depending on their age, sex, diet, habitat, season, and health condition.

Therefore, it is possible that there are larger specimens of both species that have not been recorded or discovered yet.

Another reason why it is hard to compare the size of these snakes is that they have different body shapes and proportions.

Pythons have longer and thinner bodies than anacondas, which have shorter and thicker bodies.

Pythons also have more vertebrae than anacondas, which means they can stretch more when measured.

Anacondas have more muscle mass than pythons, which means they can weigh more when measured.

Therefore, a python may appear longer than an anaconda when stretched out, but an anaconda may appear heavier than a python when coiled up.
