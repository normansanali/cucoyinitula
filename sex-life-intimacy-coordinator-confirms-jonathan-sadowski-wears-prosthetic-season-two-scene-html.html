Sex/Life intimacy coordinator confirms Jonathan Sadowski wears a prosthetic in THAT season two scene

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Friday, August 9, 2024

Sex/Life actor Jonathan Sadowski was wearing a prosthetic in the now viral nude locker room scene, the show's intimacy coordinator confirmed on Monday. 

Season two of the hit Netflix show debuted last week, and fans have been desperate to finding out if was wearing a fake penis.

In the scene itself, Jonathan, who plays Devon on the show, drops his towel and shows off his enhanced genitalia to the guys around him.

Due to the scene going viral and fans theorizing whether the moment was completely real or not, Casey Hudecki, the show’s intimacy coordinator, opened up about working on the scene.

'He was so stoked for it,' Hudecki told Us Weekly. 'I don’t wanna speak for him, but I’m sure he will tell you that he thought it was hilarious. And he was ready.'

Hudecki added, 'He had a whole prosthetic process before and after [the locker room moment].'

The moment was prompted to happen after Sadowski's character gets into a car accident and injures that part of his body.

His parts have been reconstructed, and he demonstrates a pump he has to use to get erect.

Netflix even promoted the time stamp when the moment happens after seeing how popular it became (timestamp 17 minutes 41 seconds).

Before Hudecki revealed the truth, the creator of Sex/Life, Stacy Rukeyser, spoke to Variety about the rumors.

When asked if it was a prosthetic appendage, Rukeyser said, 'I’m not sure it’s a prosthetic! We’ll never tell!'

She added that she always wanted to do a scene like that, with a genital getting enhanced surgically.

'I had done all of this research on it; I learned all about the inner penis and how that’s a real thing. I was only too eager to go down this road. It felt like kismet in that way.

'I didn’t know if Jonathan would go for it. I mean, he loves comedy, and this is ultimate comedy — but he had to really put himself out there.'

Sadowski himself has yet to comment on the scene itself and the use of a prosthetic.

Although, he did post a shot of his character from the show on his Instagram with the caption: 'Get in. I got something to show you,' the day it was released.

The actor even added a smiling devil emoji to the end of his caption.
